# Theory of Agencity

Agencity is a scalar observable intended to quantify the active structuring
capacity of a system. The theory is built around a canonical pipeline:

$$
u(\xi) \rightarrow u^*(\xi) \rightarrow X^*(\xi) \rightarrow A^*(\xi)
\rightarrow M(\xi) \rightarrow O(\xi) \rightarrow \beta(\xi) \rightarrow b(\xi)
$$

## Canonical observable

The reference observable is defined by

$$
b(\xi) = P_c(\xi)\,\frac{\beta(\xi^*) - \beta(\xi^* - \Delta^*)}{\Delta^*}
$$

where $P_c$ is the characteristic power and $\Delta^*$ is the reduced step.

## Interpretation

- $u(\xi)$ is the observed signal on a generic coordinate $\xi$
- $u^*$ is the normalized signal
- $X^*$ is the reduced activation
- $A^*$ is the reduced activity
- $M$ and $O$ encode memory and organization
- $\beta$ is the structured agencement
- $b$ is the measurable Agencity observable
